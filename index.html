-The Conference-
 
 
This one-day conference for high school and university students will explore the current status of women in Japan. Participants will assess current barriers for women and consider future opportunities for social transformation. The various speakers and panel will engage young people with the most pressing social, political, and economic issues facing women in Japan. We are honored to announce that Ms. Akie Abe will be our keynote speaker.

このコンファレンス（1日）のねらいは、高校生や大学生が日本の女性の現状を探ることです。参加者は現代の女性が抱える問題点を検討し、未来に向けての社会変革の機会を模索します。（ゲスト）スピーカーの方々は、日本の女性が直面している社会、政治、経済問題について参加者と話し合います。この度、光栄なことに基調講演に安倍昭恵さんをお迎えすることになります。
 
 
 
-About SAGE-
 
 
The American School in Japan (ASIJ) Students Advocating for Gender Equality’s (SAGE) mission is to spread awareness about gender inequality issues all around the world, while also raising money for the Asian University for Women (AUW) in Bangladesh. Last year SAGE was able to raise enough money to support the scholarship of one young woman to the Asian University for Women. SAGE is sponsoring a conference on Tuesday, March 8, 2016 at The American School in Japan in Chofu, Tokyo.

アメリカンスクール・イン・ジャパン（ASIJ）の男女共同参画推進クラブ（ SAGE ）のミッションは、世界各地にある男女不平等問題についての意識を高めることです。SAGEはバングラデシュのアジア女子大学（ AUW ）への寄附を募り、昨年はアジア女子大学生一人に奨学金を出しました。 2016年3月8日（火）、SAGEは東京都調布市アメリカンスクールにてコンファレンスを開催します。

Sachin Shah

Sachin Shah joined MetLife in 1999 and is currently the President and CEO of MetLife Japan. His prior jobs include engineering missiles and elevators. Mr. Shah has been an active supporter for the advancement of women in corporate Japan; he was a guest speaker at the TOMODACHI MetLife Women’s Leadership Program Conference and hosted the symposium “A Society Where Women Shine and My Career” with the World Assembly for Women in Tokyo. He strongly believes that diversity and inclusion are imperative in business and applies such views through his leadership at MetLife.

Kumi Sato

Kumi Sato has been the President and CEO of COSMO, an independent strategic communication consultancy, since 1987. COSMO focuses on introducing overseas products and services to the Japanese market, and Sato has often been attributed for bringing an international focus to the company. Despite the male-dominated Japanese corporate world she has been recognized as an accomplished and admirable leader; she was awarded Harvard Business School Club of Japan’s “Business Stateswoman of the Year” in 2012 and won the Asia-Pacific SABRE “Outstanding Individual Achievement” Award in 2011. In addition to her professional career she is a wife and a mother of three, a testament to her insistence that “women can have it all” A proponent of workplace diversification, she served as Chair of the American Chamber of Commerce in Japan (ACCJ) and is currently the Vice-Chair of the its Labor Diversification Task Force. Sato has written two books: Aisareru Kaisha no Joken (2007) and Communication Leadership (2012).

Renge Jibu

After graduating Hitotsubashi Law School, Renge Jibu started her career at Nikkei Business Publications. Since then, Jibu has been a journalist for various business magazines in Japan, reporting on corporate strategy, human resources, and career development. As a self proclaimed activist for female empowerment, her focus throughout her career has consistently been working women. In 2006 she was a Fulbright Visiting Scholar at the Centre for the Education of Women at the University of Michigan. During this time she conducted research about American dual-career couples with children. Upon her return to Japan she continued her studies in Japan upon her return. She has since written two books on the topic; "Kasegu Tsuma • Sodateru Otto ― Fuufu no Senryaku Teki Yakuwari Koukan” and “Futari no Kosodate Ruulu”. Jibu is also a mother of two children. In 2013, she was a researcher at the Showa Women’s University’s Modern Business Research Institute, and in 2014 she became Vice President at Toshima & Associates, where she specializes in financial content planning and product development consulting.

Miki Saito

Miki Saito, although born in Japan, has moved back and forth between Japan and the U.S her whole life . Sato studied design at KIDI Parsons in Kanazawa, Japan and Art Centre College of Design in California; she finds that her ever changing environment and subsequent exposure to different cultures and ideas has taken her art in many directions throughout her career. Now a painter, sculptor, designer, and writer, she hopes her art bridges the gap between cultures and languages. Saito has had numerous exhibitions throughout Tokyo and the U.S., and she has won the Best of Show Gary Horowitz award for her stunning work. She also works as a translator and was an Art Coordinator on the 71st Peace Boat cruise ship in 2001, working with various artists, environmentalists, peace activists, and UN officials. In her art, she often uses Japanese sumi ink and takes inspiration from the wilderness. She is known to employ the “suminagashi” process where ink is layered to create a painting.

Asako Osaki

After graduating from Sophia University, Osaki Asako pursued a master’s degree at Columbia University in International Relations, specifically International Human rights and humanitarian issues. Though she planned to become a journalist, she switched her focus to human rights after giving birth to the first of her two children; she then obtained her degree while tackling motherhood. She started her career at the United Nations Development Programme (UNDP) in New York City, where she was the specialist of the Development Policy Bureau's gender and women’s empowerment sectors. During her time there, she worked on policymaking surrounding poverty reduction, democratic governance, and conflict/disaster area reconstruction, viewing every project through a gender-aware lens. Osaka was also involved in contributing to the United Nations Millennium Development goals. In her research she illustrates how divided the world still is by gender, and believes that education is key in female empowerment. In 2004 she returned to Japan and now works as a gender specialist for government organisations, aid agencies, international organisations, NGOs, and research institutes. Osaki is also currently an Associate Professor at Kwansei Gakuin University, a specialist at Gender Action Platform (a NGO that focuses on Japanese gender equality), a part-time lecturer at the University of the Sacred Heart, director of the International Women’s Club Japan, and director of public interest at Plan Japan.

-Student Share-
 
 
Student Share is designed to give students a platform at the “Women in Japan: Realities, Challenges, and Opportunities" conference. We want to hear what issues inspire and captivate you, and your perspective on the pressing social, cultural, and political questions of our time.

「日本の女性：現状、チャンレンジ、そして未来へ」コンファレンスで、高校生や大学生に英語で意見を発表する場を与えるためにスチューデントシェアが企画されました。現代の社会的、文化的、政治的な問題に対し、どのような考えを持っているのかをぜひ聞かせてください。

To that end, we welcome student submissions for audio-visual displays, posters, or artwork. If you are making a poster, we recommend using a tri-fold board for the ease of display. If you would like to incorporate audio-visual materials in your work, such as playing a video, please specify on this form.

今回は高校生、大学生の皆さんのオーディオビジュアル、ポスター、またはアート作品を募集します。ビデオなど、オーディオビジュアルを発表をする場合はフォームに記入してください。ポスターを作成する場合は三つ折りボードを使ってください。
